Our Story

175 Years of Hospitality

From a Victorian coaching inn to Ashford's most beloved pub — discover the story behind The Copper Kettle.

Heritage

A Pub Steeped in History

The Copper Kettle first opened its doors in 1847, when Ashford was a bustling market town at the crossroads of Kent. Originally a coaching inn serving weary travellers on the London to Dover route, the pub earned its name from the gleaming copper kettle that hung above the bar — a symbol of warmth and welcome that endures to this day.

Through two world wars, the rise and fall of the railway, and decades of change, The Copper Kettle remained a constant — a place where the community gathered, stories were shared, and pints were pulled with pride.

Today, we've lovingly restored the original Victorian features while adding modern comforts, creating a space that honours the past while embracing the present.

Milestones

Key Moments in Our History

1847

Thomas and Margaret Hawthorne open The Copper Kettle as a coaching inn on Ashford High Street.

1892

The iconic copper kettle is installed above the bar — it remains there to this day, polished weekly.

1923

Survives a fire that damaged neighbouring buildings. The community rallies to rebuild and restore the pub.

1978

Listed as a Grade II building, recognising its architectural and historical significance.

2015

Current owners David and Helen Crawford take over, beginning a careful restoration of the original features.

2023

Awarded Kent Pub of the Year by the Campaign for Real Ale (CAMRA).

Our Values

What We Stand For

Quality — We source ingredients from within 30 miles wherever possible. Our beef comes from a farm in Canterbury, our vegetables from a market garden in Tenterden, and our ales from breweries across Kent.

Community — We're more than a pub — we're a gathering place. We support local charities, host community events, and sponsor Ashford's amateur football team.

Tradition — We believe in doing things properly. Real ale pulled by hand, roasts cooked to perfection, and service with a genuine smile.

Sustainability — Zero food waste policy, compostable packaging, and energy-efficient systems throughout the building.

The Team

Our passionate team works tirelessly to make every visit memorable.

Landlady & Events Manager

Sophie Reynolds

Bar Manager

Sophie manages our eight hand-pulls and curates our gin collection. A CAMRA member and qualified sommelier, she'll always recommend the perfect pint.
